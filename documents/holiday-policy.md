# Holiday Policy

## 1. Recognized Public Holidays and Pay Protections
NileTech Solutions Ltd. officially observes all paid public holidays and weekly rest days determined by the Federal Government of Ethiopia. Employees are entitled to a full day of rest on these recognized calendar dates. There shall be absolutely no reduction executed against an employee's regular monthly salary or base pay on account of having not worked on a public holiday, a weekly rest day, or on a specific day where corporate offices are closed by the express order of the Government.

The 13 official public holidays recognized under this policy are classified below:

### 1.1 National and Historical Holidays
* **Ethiopian New Year (Enkutatash)**: Observed on September 11 (or September 12 in an Ethiopian leap year).
* **Victory of Adwa Day**: Observed on March 2 to honor the historical victory over colonial forces.
* **International Labour Day**: Observed on May 1.
* **Patriots' Victory Day**: Observed on May 5 to honor the resistance against foreign occupation.

### 1.2 Christian Religious Holidays
These holidays follow the Ethiopian Orthodox (Julian) calendar, meaning their Gregorian calendar dates are fixed or shift based on local ecclesiastical determination:
* **Ethiopian Christmas (Genna)**: Observed on January 7.
* **Epiphany (Timkat)**: Observed on January 19 (or January 20 in an Ethiopian leap year).
* **Finding of the True Cross (Meskel)**: Observed on September 27 (or September 28 in an Ethiopian leap year).
* **Ethiopian Good Friday (Siklet)**: Movable holiday (Friday preceding Easter).
* **Ethiopian Easter Sunday (Fasika)**: Movable holiday (Sunday following Lent).

### 1.3 Islamic Religious Holidays
These holidays follow the Islamic Hijri (lunar) calendar. Their exact observance dates shift backwards by approximately 11 days every year relative to the Gregorian calendar and are subject to official declaration based on local moon sightings:
* **Eid al-Fitr**: Marks the conclusion of the holy month of Ramadan.
* **Eid al-Adha (Arefa)**: The feast of the sacrifice during the annual Hajj pilgrimage.
* **Mawlid (Milad un-Nabi)**: Commemorating the birthday of the Prophet Muhammad.

## 2. Compelling Work Requirements and Overtime Protocols
* **Compelling Circumstances**: In the event that urgent, compelling business operations require an employee to perform duties on an official public holiday or on a government-closed day, the employee must be granted a choice based on their personal preference: either standardized overtime pay or a compensatory day of leave.
* **Weekly Rest Day Overtime**: Any employee who is ordered by a competent supervisor to work on a standard weekly rest day (Saturday/Sunday, depending on the operational roster) due to emergency business needs shall be granted an equal compensatory day of leave during the regular working days of the immediate next week.
