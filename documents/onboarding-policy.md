# Onboarding Policy

## 1. Onboarding Requirements and Eligibility
This policy governs the formal integration and legal verification of all new hires at NileTech Solutions Ltd. No candidate shall be formally onboarded or permitted to commence work duties unless they satisfy all primary eligibility benchmarks:
* **Verification of Age**: New hires must be at least 18 years of age (subject to specific directives governing monitored youth employment between 14 and 18).
* **Competence Assurance**: Candidates must present an authorized certificate of occupational competence matching their technical job grade.
* **Legal and Medical Clearances**: New hires must submit a police background check confirming no record of corruption, fraud, or rape within the past 5 years, alongside a medical certificate from a licensed institution proving fitness for service. An HIV/AIDS test is strictly excluded from onboarding medical files.
* **Execution of Oath**: The onboarding process is completed only when the candidate formally signs the corporate Oath of Fidelity.

## 2. Probationary Period Structure
* **Initial Probation Cycle**: Every newly appointed employee shall be served with a formal letter of probation recruitment specifying their job title, grade, starting base salary, date of commencement, and comprehensive job descriptions. The standard probation period is exactly six (6) months.
* **Continuous Performance Tracking**: The direct supervisor must actively monitor and evaluate the probationer's competence. If the performance result falls below average, management may authorize a one-time extension of the probation period for an additional three (3) months. 
* **Termination or Permanency**: If the cumulative performance evaluation result is not average or higher by the end of the extended period, the employee's service shall be terminated without additional formality. If the employee records an average or higher performance result during or at the close of the probation timeframe, they shall immediately be served with a formal letter of permanent appointment.
* **Absences During Probation**: If a probationary employee is absent due to an employment injury, the probation cycle is suspended and resumes upon medical recovery. If a probationer is on maternity leave for more than one month, she shall be permitted to complete the remaining portion of her probation period immediately upon her return to service.
