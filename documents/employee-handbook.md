## 4. Compensation and Salary Protections
* **Salary Scale and Equity**: NileTech Solutions Ltd. operates a highly standardized and transparent remuneration system. Our salary structures adhere to strict equity frameworks: all organizational positions of equal value shall feature an identical base salary to honor the statutory rule of equal pay for equal work. The salary scale clearly defines the base pay, step increments, and maximum ceilings for each job grade. Salaries are distributed reliably at the end of every calendar month directly to employees or their legally authorized representatives.
* **Salary Deductions**: The monthly salary of an employee is heavily protected against arbitrary adjustments. No portion of an employee's salary shall be attached, deducted, or set off unless backed by the express written consent of the employee, a legally binding court order, or clear provisions of applicable national law.
* **Deduction Ceilings**: Any statutory or court-ordered deductions executed against an employee's monthly salary under a court order or law shall never exceed an absolute ceiling of one-third (33.3%) of the employee's total monthly salary. 
* **Clearance Requirements**: If an employee terminates their service while indebted to the company or bound by a training contract, they will not receive a professional certificate of service or official clearance until all financial obligations are fully discharged.

## 5. Performance, Increments, and Separation
* **Continuous Performance Evaluation**: Performance evaluations are conducted on a continuous, transparent, and evidence-based tracking system. The core objectives are to ensure employees discharge duties effectively regarding volume, quality, time, and cost; to identify precise training and capability needs; and to provide factual justifications for personnel decisions. Permanent employees are entitled to a salary step increment every two years, provided their performance evaluation outcomes meet or exceed standard requirements.
* **Separation due to Inefficiency**: Service may be terminated for inefficiency if an employee shows no improvement after receiving specific capacity-building training, or if their performance remains below satisfactory for three successive evaluation periods. For long-term employees with over five years of above-satisfactory results, dismissal for inefficiency is barred unless their performance drops below satisfactory for four successive evaluation periods.
* **Retrenchment Protocols**: Retrenchment occurs only if an employee's position is abolished, a branch closes, or a redundant manpower surplus is established, and they cannot be reassigned or refuse a lower grade position. If workforce reduction occurs within a department, selection for retrenchment is based strictly on performance; individuals with the lowest evaluation metrics and qualifications compared to peers in the identical position shall be retrenched first.
* **Severance Terms**: Retrenched employees who are not otherwise entitled to a pension allowance on their termination date are entitled to severance pay. This equals three months' salary for the first year of service, plus one-third of a monthly salary for each additional year of service, capped at a maximum ceiling of twelve months' total salary.
