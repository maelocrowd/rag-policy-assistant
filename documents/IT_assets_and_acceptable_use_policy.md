# IT Assets and Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. Purpose

NileTech Solutions Ltd. provides information technology (IT) assets and digital resources to support employees in carrying out their assigned responsibilities efficiently, securely, and professionally. This policy establishes the requirements for the appropriate use, protection, management, and return of Company-owned assets and systems.

The objective of this policy is to:

- Protect Company assets and information.
- Promote responsible and secure use of technology resources.
- Minimize operational, security, and compliance risks.
- Define employee responsibilities regarding Company property.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, interns, temporary staff, and any other individuals who are granted access to Company-owned or Company-managed IT assets and systems.

---

## 3. Company IT Assets

NileTech Solutions Ltd. may provide employees with the equipment, software, and services necessary to perform their duties, including but not limited to:

- Laptops and desktop computers
- Mobile phones and tablets
- Monitors, docking stations, and peripherals
- Network and communication equipment
- Software applications and subscriptions
- Cloud platforms and storage services
- Email accounts and collaboration tools
- Access credentials and authentication devices
- Any other technology resources assigned for business use

All such assets remain the exclusive property of NileTech Solutions Ltd.

---

## 4. Acceptable Use

Company IT assets shall be used primarily for legitimate business purposes.

Employees and authorized users must:

- Use Company assets responsibly and professionally.
- Comply with all Company policies and procedures.
- Protect confidential and proprietary information.
- Follow all information security requirements.
- Use only authorized software, services, and applications.
- Promptly report any security concerns or asset-related incidents.

Employees and authorized users must not:

- Install unauthorized software or hardware.
- Share passwords or access credentials.
- Use Company systems for illegal, fraudulent, offensive, or unethical activities.
- Bypass or disable security controls.
- Access, distribute, or store content that violates applicable laws or Company policies.
- Use Company resources in a manner that disrupts business operations or compromises security.

Reasonable personal use may be permitted provided that it does not interfere with work duties, violate Company policies, consume excessive resources, or expose the Company to legal or security risks.

---

## 5. Information Security Responsibilities

All users are responsible for safeguarding Company systems, data, and technology resources.

Users must:

- Maintain strong and confidential passwords.
- Use multi-factor authentication where required.
- Lock devices when unattended.
- Keep security updates enabled and installed.
- Store Company information only on approved systems and platforms.
- Exercise caution against phishing attempts, malware, and social engineering attacks.
- Protect customer, employee, and Company confidential information.

Any suspected security incident, unauthorized access, malware infection, data breach, or system vulnerability must be reported immediately to the Information Technology Department.

---

## 6. Care, Custody, and Protection of Assets

Employees are expected to exercise reasonable care in handling Company assets.

This includes:

- Protecting equipment from theft, loss, damage, and misuse.
- Following manufacturer and Company usage guidelines.
- Securing devices during travel and remote work.
- Preventing unauthorized access to Company equipment and data.
- Maintaining assigned equipment in good working condition.

Loss, theft, damage, or malfunction of Company assets must be reported promptly to the employee's supervisor and the Information Technology Department.

---

## 7. Software and Licensing

All software used on Company devices must be approved, properly licensed, and authorized by NileTech Solutions Ltd.

Employees shall not:

- Install unapproved software or applications.
- Copy or distribute licensed software without authorization.
- Use pirated, unlicensed, or illegally obtained software.
- Share Company software licenses with unauthorized individuals.

The Company reserves the right to remove unauthorized software from its systems at any time.

---

## 8. Monitoring and Privacy

Company-owned devices, systems, networks, email accounts, and digital resources are provided for business purposes and may be monitored, logged, audited, or reviewed by NileTech Solutions Ltd. to ensure security, compliance, operational integrity, and business continuity, subject to applicable laws and regulations.

Users should not expect complete privacy when using Company-owned systems or resources.

---

## 9. Loss, Damage, and Liability

Employees may be held accountable for the loss, theft, damage, or destruction of Company assets resulting from:

- Negligence
- Misuse
- Unauthorized activities
- Intentional misconduct
- Violation of Company policies

The Company may investigate incidents and determine appropriate corrective, disciplinary, or recovery measures in accordance with applicable laws and internal procedures.

Employees shall not be held liable for normal wear and tear resulting from authorized and reasonable use of Company equipment.

---

## 10. Return of Assets and Clearance Procedures

Upon resignation, termination, contract completion, transfer, extended leave, or upon request by the Company, employees must immediately return all Company-owned assets in their possession.

Employees must cooperate with all asset verification, data transfer, account deactivation, and clearance procedures.

Final employment clearance, certificate of service, and any outstanding employment documentation may be subject to the return of all assigned Company assets and fulfillment of applicable obligations in accordance with Company procedures and applicable laws.

---

## 11. Policy Violations

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, suspension of access privileges, financial liability where permitted by law, termination of employment or contractual relationship, and legal action where appropriate.

---

## 12. Exceptions

Any exception to this policy must be approved in writing by the relevant Department Head and the Information Technology Department.

---

## 13. Policy Review and Amendments

NileTech Solutions Ltd. reserves the right to amend, revise, or update this policy at any time to address operational, technological, security, legal, or regulatory requirements.
