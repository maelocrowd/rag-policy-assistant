# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

## 1. Annual Leave Administration
The primary purpose of annual leave is to enable employees to obtain rest and resume work with renewed strength. There shall be no financial payment executed in lieu of annual leave, except when an employee's service is terminated with unused leave balances.

* **Service Eligibility**: A newly appointed employee is not entitled to take annual leave until they have completed eleven (11) consecutive months of service. After 11 months, leave may be granted in proportion to the service rendered within the budget year.
* **Leave Accumulation Scale**: Employees are entitled to a base of twenty (20) working days of annual leave for their first year of service. For every additional year of service, the employee receives one (1) additional working day, up to a maximum statutory ceiling of thirty (30) working days. Previous service rendered in other compliant federal or regional institutions is fully factored into this length of service.
* **Salary Advance**: Employees are entitled to an advance payment of their monthly salary at the time of taking their scheduled annual leave. 
* **Postponement Limits**: The company may authorize the postponement of annual leave for up to two successive budget years due to compelling operational pressures. Any such accumulated leave must be legally granted and fully utilized in the third budget year. If leave is postponed for two years, the employee may claim payment for the first year of the accumulated leave from the allocated budget, calculated by dividing the monthly salary by 30 days.

## 2. Family and Specialized Leaves
* **Maternity Leave**: A pregnant employee is entitled to fully paid leave for medical examinations and prenatal rest if recommended by a doctor. She is entitled to a total of 120 consecutive days of paid maternity leave: 30 days of prenatal leave preceding her presumed date of delivery and 90 days of post-confinement leave. If delivery occurs before the prenatal leave is exhausted, the remaining days are automatically added to the post-confinement period.
* **Miscarriage Protections**: In the event of a confirmed miscarriage after at least six months of pregnancy, an employee is entitled to 60 days of post-confinement leave. A miscarriage occurring between three and six months of pregnancy entitles the employee to 30 consecutive days of paid leave, provided it is confirmed by a medical certificate.
* **Child Treatment Leave**: Female employees shall, when confirmed by a medical certificate, be entitled to leave with pay for the time spent in the follow-up of medical treatment of their child who has not attained the age of one year.
* **Paternity Leave**: Male employees are entitled to ten (10) working days of fully paid paternity leave at the time of their wife's delivery.
* **Sick Leave**: Employees who have completed probation are entitled to a maximum of eight (8) months of sick leave in a single year, or twelve (12) months across a four-year cycle, for illnesses preventing work. Sick leave is fully paid for the first six months and paid at half-salary for the final two months. Employees must notify the company immediately and produce a medical certificate for absences exceeding three consecutive days or six total days within a budget year. Probationary staff are limited to 1 month of paid sick leave.
* **Personal Leave**: Employees are entitled to up to seven (7) days of paid leave per budget year for personal matters such as mourning, weddings, or examinations.
* **Special Leaves**: Employees are entitled to special leave with pay when summoned by a court or competent authority (for the duration utilized) and for cases involving popular elections (for the duration of the election). Special leave without pay may be authorized for up to one year on justifiable grounds if it does not adversely affect the institution.
